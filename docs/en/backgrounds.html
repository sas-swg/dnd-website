Backgrounds

Who your character was before becoming an adventurer.

🔍
Acolyte Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
Skills: Insight, Religion | Feat: Magic Initiate (Cleric)

Acolytes have experience with temple devotion, an understanding of religion and magical rites, and an initial notion of channeling divine power. This background is an ideal choice for those who want to lightly touch on divine magic and its atmosphere without multiclassing into Cleric.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma
  • Skills: Insight, Religion
  • Tools: Calligrapher's Supplies
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Magic Initiate (Cleric)
  • Equipment: Calligrapher's Supplies, Prayer Book, Holy Symbol, Parchment (10 sheets), Robe, 8 gp (or 50 gp at start).
Artisan Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence
Skills: Investigation, Persuasion | Feat: Crafter

The Artisan confirms that the character previously worked as an apprentice, whether forging weapons as a blacksmith or helping in the kitchen as a cook. This grants practical skills for crafting items.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence
  • Skills: Investigation, Persuasion
  • Tools: One type of artisan's tools of your choice
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Crafter
  • Equipment: Artisan's Tools, Bullseye Lantern, Pouch (2), Tent, Traveler's Clothes, 25 gp (or 50 gp at start).
Charlatan Dexterity, Constitution, Charisma
Skills: Deception, Sleight of Hand | Feat: Skilled

Before their adventuring days, the Charlatan earned a living through lies, perhaps playing cards with marked decks or selling fake healing potions. This is an ideal choice for characters focused on social interaction or deception.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Dexterity, Constitution, Charisma
  • Skills: Deception, Sleight of Hand
  • Tools: A gaming set (e.g., dice), Forgery Kit
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Skilled
  • Equipment: Fine Clothes, Forgery Kit, Gaming Set, 15 gp (or 50 gp at start).
Criminal Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence
Skills: Stealth, Sleight of Hand | Feat: Alert

Criminals survived through theft, smuggling, or other illegal dealings. They work superbly in the shadows and know how to react quickly to danger.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence
  • Skills: Stealth, Sleight of Hand
  • Tools: Thieves' Tools, a Gaming Set
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Alert
  • Equipment: Thieves' Tools, Dagger (2), Dark hooded clothes, 16 gp (or 50 gp at start).
Entertainer Strength, Dexterity, Charisma
Skills: Acrobatics, Performance | Feat: Musician

Entertainers are used to being the center of attention, amusing the crowd. Their performance and acrobatics skills make them a great choice for Bards and other social classes.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Strength, Dexterity, Charisma
  • Skills: Acrobatics, Performance
  • Tools: A musical instrument (of your choice)
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Musician
  • Equipment: Costume, Musical Instrument, Perfume, Traveler's Clothes, 19 gp (or 50 gp at start).
Farmer Strength, Constitution, Wisdom
Skills: Animal Handling, Nature | Feat: Tough

Farmers lived simple lives, working the land or tending livestock. This hard physical labor made them hardy and gave them knowledge of nature.

Basics:
  • Ability Scores (+3): Strength, Constitution, Wisdom
  • Skills: Animal Handling, Nature
  • Tools: Carpenter's Tools
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Tough — grants extra HP.
  • Equipment: Carpenter's Tools, Iron Pot, Sickle, Tent, Traveler's Clothes, 23 gp (or 50 gp at start).

Soldier Strength, Dexterity, Constitution
Skills: Athletics, Intimidation | Feat: Savage Attacker

The Soldier is a natural choice for a Fighter or Barbarian. They know the realities of the battlefield, which made them strong and formidable. Even for a Cleric this can be an interesting choice (for example, a soldier who turned to religion after the war).

Wayfarer Dexterity, Wisdom, Charisma
Skills: Insight, Stealth | Feat: Lucky

They are used to living on the streets, with no home or people to rely on. Like criminals, they are used to surviving against the odds, relying only on their luck and stealth.

  • Skills: Insight, Stealth
  • Tools: Thieves' Tools
Equipment & Bonuses:
  • Starting Feat: Lucky
  • Equipment: Two daggers, Thieves' Tools, Bedroll, Two pouches, Traveler's Clothes, 16 gp (or 50 gp at start).

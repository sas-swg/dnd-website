Key Class Features

Rage - Level 1

You can enter a Rage as a Bonus Action, if you are not wearing heavy armor. You gain the following benefits:

  • Advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
  • A bonus to melee weapon damage rolls (shown in the table) that use Strength, or unarmed strikes.
  • Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage.
  • You can't maintain Concentration or cast spells.

Your Rage lasts until the end of your next turn. If you are raging on your next turn, you can extend it for another round by attacking an enemy, forcing it to make a saving throw, or spending a Bonus Action. You can maintain a Rage for up to 10 minutes.

Unarmored Defense - Level 1

While you are not wearing any armor, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Constitution modifier. You can still use a shield and gain this benefit.

Weapon Mastery - Level 1

Choose 2 types of Simple or Martial weapons. You can use their Mastery properties in combat. After a Long Rest you can change one of these weapon types to another.

Danger Sense - Level 2

You have Advantage on Dexterity saving throws, unless you are Incapacitated.

Reckless Attack - Level 2

When making your first attack on your turn, you can attack recklessly. This gives you advantage (advantage) on all melee weapon attacks using Strength until the start of your next turn. However, all enemy attacks against YOU are also made with advantage during this time.

Primal Knowledge - Level 3

You gain proficiency in one more skill from the Barbarian's initial list. Also, while you are Raging, you can use your Strength (instead of the normal ability) for checks: Acrobatics, Intimidation, Perception, Stealth, and Survival.

Extra Attack - Level 5

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action.

Fast Movement - Level 5

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren't wearing heavy armor.

Wild Instinct - Level 7

You have Advantage on Initiative rolls.

Instinctive Pounce - Level 7

As part of the Bonus Action you use to enter your Rage, you can move up to half your speed.

Brutal Strike - Level 9

If you use Reckless Attack, you can forgo the "advantage" on one attack. If it hits, you deal +1d10 damage and choose an effect: push the target 15 feet or reduce its speed by 15 feet. (At 13th level adds Topple or Ally Bonus). (At 17th level damage increases to 2d10).

Relentless Rage - Level 11

If you drop to 0 HP while Raging, you can make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10). On a success you gain HP equal to your Barbarian level × 2. Each subsequent use increases the DC by 5.

Persistent Rage - Level 15

When you roll Initiative, you can recover all expended uses of Rage (once per Long Rest). Your Rage now automatically lasts 10 minutes without needing to be extended.

Indomitable Might - Level 18

If the result of a Strength check or saving throw is lower than your Strength score, you can use that score instead of the result.

Primal Champion - Level 20

You embody primal power. Your Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4, and their maximums are now 25.

Key Class Features

Bardic Inspiration - Level 1

You can inspire others through stirring words or music. As a Bonus Action choose another creature within 60 ft that can hear you. That creature gains a Bardic Inspiration die, starting with d6.

For the next 1 hour that creature can add this die to one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. This can be done after the d20 is rolled but before the DM announces the result.

You have a number of uses equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). You regain them after a Long Rest.

Spellcasting - Level 1

You cast spells from the Bard spell list, using Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You can use any musical instrument as a spellcasting focus.

Expertise - Levels 2 and 9

Choose 2 skills in which you are proficient. Your Proficiency Bonus for those skills doubles. At 9th level you may choose 2 more skills.

Jack of All Trades - Level 2

You can add half your Proficiency Bonus (rounded down) to any Ability Check that does not already include your Proficiency Bonus. (Note: Initiative is a Dexterity check, so this applies to it too!).

Font of Inspiration - Level 5

You now regain all expended uses of Bardic Inspiration after finishing a Short or Long Rest. You can also spend a spell slot (no action required) to regain one use.

Countercharm - Level 7

When a creature within 30 ft (including you) must make a save against Charmed or Frightened, you can use your Reaction to give it Advantage on that roll.

Magical Secrets - Level 10

You have uncovered the secrets of other magical traditions. Whenever you gain a Bard level and choose a new spell, you can pick it from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard list (it counts as a Bard spell for you).

Superior Inspiration - Level 18

If you roll Initiative and have no Bardic Inspiration charges remaining, you automatically regain 2 charges.

Words of Creation - Level 20

You always have Power Word Heal and Power Word Kill prepared. When you cast them, you can target two creatures simultaneously (if they are within 10 feet of each other).

Key Class Features

Spellcasting - Level 1

Your magic comes from divine power. You use Wisdom for spellcasting and a Holy Symbol as your focus. Clerics prepare their spell list each day during a Long Rest.

Divine Order - Level 1

You choose your style of service. Protector: You gain proficiency with Martial weapons and Heavy armor. Thaumaturge: You gain an extra Cantrip and add your Wisdom modifier to Religion and Arcana checks.

Channel Divinity - Level 2

You can channel divine energy. You have 2 uses (recharge after a Short or Long Rest). You gain base effects plus additional ones from your subclass at 3rd level:

  • Divine Spark: As an Action, direct a ray of light at a creature within 30 ft. Either heal it for 1d8 + Wisdom mod, or force a Constitution save (on a fail it takes the same amount as Necrotic or Radiant damage). The number of d8 increases at levels 7 (2d8), 13 (3d8), and 18 (4d8).
  • Turn Undead: As an Action, you present your Holy Symbol. Each undead within 30 ft makes a Wisdom save. On a fail it becomes Frightened and must flee from you for 1 minute.

Destroy Undead - Level 5

When you use Turn Undead, you roll a number of d8s equal to your Wisdom modifier. Each undead that fails its save takes Radiant damage equal to the roll total. This doesn't remove the Turn Undead effect!

Blessed Strikes - Level 7

Choose one: Divine Strike (once per turn when hitting with a weapon, deal +1d8 Radiant/Necrotic damage) OR Potent Spellcasting (add your Wisdom modifier to Cantrip damage).

Divine Intervention - Level 10

As a Magic Action you can automatically cast ANY spell from the Cleric spell list of 5th level or lower, without expending a slot or components. You cannot use this ability again until you finish a Long Rest.

Improved Blessed Strikes - Level 14

If you have Divine Strike: damage increases to 2d8. If Potent Spellcasting: when you deal Cantrip damage, you or an ally within 60 ft gain Temporary HP equal to double your Wisdom modifier.

Greater Divine Intervention - Level 20

When you use Divine Intervention, you can choose the spell Wish from the spell list. If you do, you cannot use Divine Intervention again for 2d4 Long Rests.

Key Class Features

Spellcasting and Language - Level 1

You use Wisdom for spellcasting and can use a Druidic focus (e.g. a sprig of mistletoe). Druids prepare their spell list each day during a Long Rest.

Druidic: You know the secret language of druids, which can be used to leave hidden messages. Through it you always have Speak with Animals prepared.

Primal Order - Level 1

Choose your role:
Magician: You know 1 additional Cantrip. You gain a bonus to Arcana or Nature checks equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum +1).
Warden: You gain proficiency with Martial weapons and Medium armor.

Wild Shape - Level 2

As a Bonus Action, you can transform into a Beast form you know. You know 4 Beast forms (they can be changed after a Long Rest).

  • Form limitations: At 2nd level max CR 1/4, no flying speed. At 4th level max CR 1/2. At 8th level max CR 1, and you can fly.
  • Duration: A number of hours equal to half your Druid level.
  • Hit Points: You take on the beast's stats but keep your mental scores (INT, WIS, CHA) and skills. When you transform you gain Temporary HP equal to your Druid level.

Wild Companion - Level 2

As a Magic action, you can expend a spell slot OR one Wild Shape use to cast Find Familiar (no material components). The familiar has the Fey type and disappears after a Long Rest.

Wild Resurgence - Level 5

Once on your turn, if you have no Wild Shape uses, you can sacrifice a spell slot to regain 1 use. You can also sacrifice a Wild Shape once per day to regain a 1st-level spell slot.

Elemental Fury - Level 7

Choose one: Potent Spellcasting (add Wisdom modifier to Cantrip damage) OR Primal Strike (once per turn when hitting with a weapon or beast attack, deal an extra 1d8 Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Thunder damage).

Improved Elemental Fury - Level 15

Spellcasting: Cantrips with a range of 10+ ft increase their range by 300 feet. Strike: The extra damage increases to 2d8.

Beast Spells - Level 18

While in Wild Shape (as an animal), you can cast spells that don't require material components with a listed cost.

Archdruid - Level 20

You age ten times more slowly. When you roll Initiative and have 0 Wild Shape uses, you regain 1 use. You can also convert Wild Shape charges into spell slots (1 charge = 2 spell levels).

Key Class Features

Second Wind - Level 1

You have a limited reservoir of stamina. As a Bonus Action, you can use it to regain HP equal to 1d10 + your Fighter level.

You regain one expended use after a Short Rest and all uses after a Long Rest. The maximum uses increase at levels 4 and 10.

Weapon Mastery - Level 1

Choose 3 types of Simple or Martial weapons. You can use their Mastery properties in combat. After a Long Rest you can change one weapon type to another. The number of weapons increases with level.

Fighting Style - Level 1

You gain a Fighting Style feat of your choice (for example: Archery, Dueling, Defense, etc.). You can change this style whenever you gain a new Fighter level.

Action Surge - Level 2

On your turn you can take one additional action (other than a Magic action). Recharges after a Short or Long Rest. (At 17th level: 2 uses).

Tactical Mind - Level 2

When you fail an ability check, you can expend a Second Wind use. Instead of healing, you roll 1d10 and add it to the check. If it still fails, Second Wind is not expended!

Extra Attack - Levels 5, 11, 20

You can attack twice (level 5), three times (level 11), or four times (level 20) when you take the Attack action.

Tactical Shift - Level 5

When you activate Second Wind as a Bonus Action, you can move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

Tactical Master - Level 9

When you attack with a weapon whose Mastery property you can use, you can replace that property with Push, Sap, or Slow for that attack.

Indomitable - Level 9

If you fail a saving throw, you can reroll it with a bonus equal to your Fighter level (you must use the new roll). Once per Long Rest (twice at 13th level, three times at 17th).

Studied Attacks - Level 13

You study your opponents. If you make an attack roll against a creature and miss, you gain Advantage on your next attack roll against that creature until the end of your next turn.

Pact Magic - Level 1

Your magic is unique. You use Charisma as your spellcasting ability. Taunting Step: one creature within 5 ft of the destination has Disadvantage on attack rolls against creatures other than you, until the start of your next turn.

  • Level 6: Misty Escape. All your Warlock spell slots are the highest level available (maximum 5th level). When you cast a spell of any level, it automatically uses this slot and is upcast to its level.
  • You have Immunity to Charmed. When a creature tries to Charm you, you can reflect it: use your Reaction to make that creature Charmed by you for 1 minute (Wisdom save cancels it). (Once per Short/Long Rest or expend a Pact Slot.) You regain ALL expended spell slots after finishing a Short or Long Rest.

Eldritch Invocations - Level 1

You learn fragments of forbidden knowledge that grant you permanent magical abilities. You gain 1 Invocation at level 1, and the count grows as you level. Once per Long Rest, expend a Pact Slot to cast Confusion without a slot.

  • Pact of the Blade: You create a magical weapon. You can use Charisma instead of Strength or Dexterity for attack and damage rolls with it.
  • Pact of the Tome: You gain a magical book that gives you 3 extra cantrips and 2 ritual spells (and you can cast those rituals faster).
  • Pact of the Chain: You gain an enhanced otherworldly familiar (Imp, Quasit, Sprite, etc.) that can attack on your behalf.
  • Agonizing Blast: Adds your Charisma modifier to the damage of the Eldritch Blast cantrip.

You can spend 1 minute in a magical trance to regain half your expended Pact Magic slots (rounded up). Once per Long Rest.

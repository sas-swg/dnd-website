Barbarian Untamed fury and primal strength
Hit Die: 1d12 | Primary: Strength

Barbarians are mighty warriors empowered by the primal forces of the multiverse, manifested as Rage. More than just an emotion, this Rage is an embodiment of a predator's ferocity, the fury of a storm, and the turbulence of the sea. Barbarians throw themselves headlong into danger to protect those beside them.

  • At Higher Levels: 1d12 (or 7) + Con modifier

  • Armor: Light, Medium armor, Shields
  • Weapons:Martial
  • Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution
  • Skills (choose 2): Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival

Key Class Features

Rage - Level 1

You can enter a Rage as a Bonus Action, if you are not wearing heavy armor. You gain the following benefits:

  • Advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.
  • A bonus to melee weapon damage rolls (shown in the table) that use Strength, or unarmed strikes.
  • Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage.
  • You can't maintain Concentration or cast spells.

Your Rage lasts until the end of your next turn. If you are raging on your next turn, you can extend it for another round by attacking an enemy, forcing it to make a saving throw, or spending a Bonus Action. You can maintain a Rage for up to 10 minutes.

Unarmored Defense - Level 1

While you are not wearing any armor, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Constitution modifier. You can still use a shield and gain this benefit.

Weapon Mastery - Level 1

Choose 2 types of Simple or Martial weapons. You can use their Mastery properties in combat. After a Long Rest you can change one of these weapon types to another.

Danger Sense - Level 2

You have Advantage on Dexterity saving throws, unless you are Incapacitated.

Reckless Attack - Level 2

When making your first attack on your turn, you can attack recklessly. This gives you advantage (advantage) on all melee weapon attacks using Strength until the start of your next turn. However, all enemy attacks against YOU are also made with advantage during this time.

Primal Knowledge - Level 3

You gain proficiency in one more skill from the Barbarian's initial list. Also, while you are Raging, you can use your Strength (instead of the normal ability) for checks: Acrobatics, Intimidation, Perception, Stealth, and Survival.

Extra Attack - Level 5

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action.

Fast Movement - Level 5

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren't wearing heavy armor.

Wild Instinct - Level 7

You have Advantage on Initiative rolls.

Instinctive Pounce - Level 7

As part of the Bonus Action you use to enter your Rage, you can move up to half your speed.

Brutal Strike - Level 9

If you use Reckless Attack, you can forgo the "advantage" on one attack. If it hits, you deal +1d10 damage and choose an effect: push the target 15 feet or reduce its speed by 15 feet. (At 13th level adds Topple or Ally Bonus). (At 17th level damage increases to 2d10).

Relentless Rage - Level 11

If you drop to 0 HP while Raging, you can make a Constitution saving throw (DC 10). On a success you gain HP equal to your Barbarian level × 2. Each subsequent use increases the DC by 5.

Persistent Rage - Level 15

When you roll Initiative, you can recover all expended uses of Rage (once per Long Rest). Your Rage now automatically lasts 10 minutes without needing to be extended.

Indomitable Might - Level 18

If the result of a Strength check or saving throw is lower than your Strength score, you can use that score instead of the result.

Primal Champion - Level 20

You embody primal power. Your Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4, and their maximums are now 25.

Barbarian Subclasses

At 3rd level you choose a Path that shapes your rage.

Path of the Berserker

For those who surrender to bloodlust and unrestrained violence.

Level 3: Frenzy. While you are Raging and using Reckless Attack, the first target you hit on your turn takes extra damage equal to a number of d6 matching your Rage damage bonus (e.g. 2d6).
Level 6: Mindless Rage. You have Immunity to Charmed and Frightened, while you are Raging. If those conditions were already on you when you entered Rage, they end.
Level 10: Retaliation. When you take damage from a creature within 5 feet, you can use your Reaction to make one melee attack (weapon or unarmed) against it.
Level 14: Intimidating Presence. As a Bonus Action, you invoke dread through your presence. Chosen creatures within 30 ft make a Wisdom save. On a fail they are Frightened for 1 minute. They can repeat the save at the end of each of their turns. (Once per Long Rest, or restore by expending a Rage use).
Path of the Wild Heart

Kinship with animals, drawing on their powers in battle.

Level 3: Animal Speaker. You can cast Beast Sense and Speak with Animals as Rituals.
Rage of the Wilds: When you enter Rage, choose an option. Bear: Resistance to all damage except Force, Necrotic, Psychic, and Radiant. Eagle: Dash and Disengage as part of entering Rage. Can use both as a Bonus Action during Rage. Wolf: Your allies have advantage on melee attacks against enemies within 5 ft of you.
Level 6: Aspect of the Wild. Changes after a Long Rest. Owl: Darkvision 60 ft, or +60 ft to existing. Panther: Climbing speed equals your walking speed. Salmon: Swimming speed equals your walking speed.
Level 10: Nature Speaker. You can cast Commune with Nature as a Ritual.
Level 14: Power of the Wilds. Choose during Rage. Falcon: Flying speed if you have no armor. Lion: Enemies within 5 ft of you have Disadvantage on attacks against anyone except you. Ram: You can knock a Large (or smaller) creature prone with a melee attack.
Path of the World Tree

A connection to the cosmic tree Yggdrasil, which nourishes life and channels the magic of space.

Level 3: Vitality of the Tree. When you enter Rage, you gain Temporary HP equal to your Barbarian level. At the start of each of your turns in Rage, you can give another creature within 10 ft Temporary HP (amount = d6 dice equal to your Rage bonus).
Level 6: Branches of the Tree. When an enemy you can see starts its turn within 30 ft of you during your Rage, you can use your Reaction to summon spectral branches. The enemy makes a Strength save; on a fail it is teleported 5 ft away from you, and you reduce its speed to 0 until end of turn.
Level 10: Battering Roots. During your turn, the reach of any Heavy or Versatile melee weapon increases by 10 feet via tree tendrils. When you hit, you can additionally apply the Push or Topple property.
Level 14: Travel along the Tree. When you activate Rage (and as a Bonus Action during it), you can teleport 60 ft to a visible free space. Once per Rage you can extend the range to 150 ft and bring up to 6 willing creatures within 10 ft of you.
Path of the Zealot

Fanatical warriors led into battle by the gods. They experience Rage as a divine union.

Level 3: Divine Fury. The first attack each turn that hits while Raging deals extra damage (Necrotic or Radiant) equal to 1d6 + half your Barbarian level.
Warrior of the Gods: You have a pool of four d12 dice (increases at higher levels). As a Bonus Action you can expend these dice, roll them, and heal yourself for that amount. Restores during a Long Rest.
Level 6: Fanatical Focus. Once per active Rage, if you fail a saving throw, you can reroll it with a bonus equal to your Rage damage bonus.
Level 10: Zealous Presence. As a Bonus Action, you unleash a battle cry. Up to 10 creatures within 60 ft gain Advantage on attacks and saves until the start of your next turn. (Once per Long Rest, or restore by expending a Rage use).
Level 14: Rage of the Gods. When you enter Rage, you can assume a divine warrior form (1 minute or until you drop to 0 HP). Grants Flight (speed equals yours), Resistance to Necrotic, Psychic, and Radiant damage. When a creature within 30 ft drops to 0 HP, you can use your Reaction to expend a Rage use to give it HP equal to your Barbarian level. (Once per Long Rest).
Path of the Muscle Wizard

You are a wizard! With gigantic, bulging muscles and a dumb hat that "casts" magic with fists.

Level 3: Irrefutable Wizardry. Advantage on Intimidation to convince others you are a wizard. If anyone doubts, you can use your Reaction to enter Rage until end of next turn (does not expend uses).

Level 3: "Cantrips". Once per turn on a hit using a Strength-based attack:
Mage Hand: Push target 5 ft (10 ft while Raging).
Shocking Grasp: Target can’t make Opportunity Attacks until the end of this turn (or until your next turn while Raging).
True Strike: Extra 1d6 damage (while Raging, add half your Barbarian level too).
Level 6: "Spells". During Rage you can use each "spell" once (recharges after a Long Rest):
Burning Hands: As an Action make an Unarmed Attack against everyone in reach. Deals 1d8+Strength, and the target has Disadvantage on its next attack.
Magic Missile: As an Action, make 3 thrown Strength attacks with Advantage (they never miss!).
Shield: As a Reaction, put on a shield. Adds to AC. If the attack hits, damage is reduced by your Barbarian level.

Level 10: Magic Resistance. Advantage on all magical saving throws, while you are Raging.

Level 14: I Cast FIST. During Rage's Attack action, replace one strike with a crazy fist punch with Advantage. Deals 6d6+Strength damage and knocks the target prone.

Key Class Features

Bardic Inspiration - Level 1

You can inspire others through stirring words or music. As a Bonus Action choose another creature within 60 ft that can hear you. That creature gains a Bardic Inspiration die, starting with d6.

For the next 1 hour that creature can add this die to one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw. This can be done after the d20 is rolled but before the DM announces the result.

You have a number of uses equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1). You regain them after a Long Rest.

Spellcasting - Level 1

You cast spells from the Bard spell list, using Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You can use any musical instrument as a spellcasting focus.

Expertise - Levels 2 and 9

Choose 2 skills in which you are proficient. Your Proficiency Bonus for those skills doubles. At 9th level you may choose 2 more skills.

Jack of All Trades - Level 2

You can add half your Proficiency Bonus (rounded down) to any Ability Check that does not already include your Proficiency Bonus. (Note: Initiative is a Dexterity check, so this applies to it too!).

Font of Inspiration - Level 5

You now regain all expended uses of Bardic Inspiration after finishing a Short or Long Rest. You can also spend a spell slot (no action required) to regain one use.

Countercharm - Level 7

When a creature within 30 ft (including you) must make a save against Charmed or Frightened, you can use your Reaction to give it Advantage on that roll.

Magical Secrets - Level 10

You have uncovered the secrets of other magical traditions. Whenever you gain a Bard level and choose a new spell, you can pick it from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard list (it counts as a Bard spell for you).

Superior Inspiration - Level 18

If you roll Initiative and have no Bardic Inspiration charges remaining, you automatically regain 2 charges.

Words of Creation - Level 20

You always have Power Word Heal and Power Word Kill prepared. When you cast them, you can target two creatures simultaneously (if they are within 10 feet of each other).

Bard Colleges

At 3rd level you choose a College where you hone your skills and performance style.

College of Dance

Masters of deadly and graceful unarmed combat who shun weapons and armor.

Level 3: Dazzling Footwork.
Tandem: Your unarmored AC equals 10 + Dexterity + Charisma.
Bard's Strike: You can make Unarmed Strikes using Dexterity instead of Strength. Damage equals your Bardic Inspiration die + Dexterity.
Agility: When you use Bardic Inspiration (bonus action), you can immediately make an Unarmed Strike. You can also move up to half your speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Level 3: Inspiring Movement. When an enemy ends its turn within 5 ft of your ally (and you can see this), you can use your Reaction and expend Inspiration. You and the ally can each move up to half their speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Level 6: Tandem Footwork. When Initiative is rolled, you can expend 1 Bardic Inspiration. You and your allies (within 60 ft) add the result of that die to their Initiative.
Level 14: Leading Evasion. When you or an ally within 5 ft makes a Dexterity save (e.g. from Fireball) for half damage, they take no damage on a success and only half on a failure.
College of Glamour

Fey magic that captivates minds, alters perception, and invokes wonder.

Level 3: Beguiling Magic. You always have Charm Person and Mirror Image. After casting any Illusion or Enchantment spell, you can automatically cast Charm Person for free (no slot or components).
Level 3: Mantle of Inspiration. As a Bonus Action, expend Bardic Inspiration to appear incredibly charming. Choose allies (up to your Charisma mod). They gain Temporary HP equal to twice the Inspiration die roll. They can also use their Reaction to immediately move up to their speed without provoking opportunity attacks.
Level 6: Mantle of Majesty. You always have the spell Command. You can cast it as a Bonus Action without expending a slot. For 1 minute afterward you look majestic and can cast Command for FREE each of your turns as a Bonus Action (recharges after a Long Rest).
Level 14: Unbreakable Majesty. You always have the spell Sanctuary. As a Bonus Action you can assume a magical bearing for 1 minute. Whenever a creature attacks you, it makes a Charisma save. On a fail the attack misses and it must choose a new target. On a success it hits, but has Disadvantage on all saves against YOU on its next turn.
College of Lore

Collectors of ancient secrets and powerful spells from every corner of the world.

Level 3: Bonus Proficiencies. You gain proficiency in 3 more skills of your choice.
Level 3: Cutting Words. When an enemy within 60 ft (that can hear you) makes an attack roll, ability check, or damage roll, you can use your Reaction to expend Bardic Inspiration. Subtract the result from their roll. (You can save an ally from critical damage or turn a hit into a miss).
Level 6: Magical Discoveries. You learn 2 additional spells (of any level you can cast) from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard spell list. They count as Bard spells and do not count against your known spells limit.
Level 14: Peerless Skill. When you make an ability check and fail, you can expend 1 Bardic Inspiration and add the result to your roll (potentially turning a failure into a success). If the check still fails, the Inspiration is not expended!
College of Valor

Heroic skalds who not only sing of heroes past but fight alongside them.

Level 3: Combat Training. You gain proficiency with Martial weapons, Medium armor, and Shields. You can use a weapon as a spellcasting focus.
Level 3: Combat Inspiration. Allies you have given Inspiration can use it in additional ways:
1. When the ally attacks and hits, they can add the die to their damage.
2. When an ally is hit by an attack, they can use their Reaction to roll the die and add the result to their AC, which may turn the attack into a miss.
Level 6: Extra Attack. You can attack twice instead of once when you take the Attack action. You can replace one of those attacks with a Cantrip that has a casting time of 1 action.
Level 14: Battle Magic. After you cast a spell that requires 1 action, you can make one melee or ranged weapon attack as a Bonus Action on the same turn.

Key Class Features

Spellcasting - Level 1

Your magic comes from divine power. You use Wisdom for spellcasting and a Holy Symbol as your focus. Clerics prepare their spell list each day during a Long Rest.

Divine Order - Level 1

You choose your style of service. Protector: You gain proficiency with Martial weapons and Heavy armor. Thaumaturge: You gain an extra Cantrip and add your Wisdom modifier to Religion and Arcana checks.

Channel Divinity - Level 2

You can channel divine energy. You have 2 uses (recharge after a Short or Long Rest). You gain base effects plus additional ones from your subclass at 3rd level:

  • Divine Spark: As an Action, direct a ray of light at a creature within 30 ft. Either heal it for 1d8 + Wisdom mod, or force a Constitution save (on a fail it takes the same amount as Necrotic or Radiant damage). The number of d8 increases at levels 7 (2d8), 13 (3d8), and 18 (4d8).
  • Turn Undead: As an Action, you present your Holy Symbol. Each undead within 30 ft makes a Wisdom save. On a fail it becomes Frightened and must flee from you for 1 minute.

Destroy Undead - Level 5

When you use Turn Undead, you roll a number of d8s equal to your Wisdom modifier. Each undead that fails its save takes Radiant damage equal to the roll total. This doesn't remove the Turn Undead effect!

Blessed Strikes - Level 7

Choose one: Divine Strike (once per turn when hitting with a weapon, deal +1d8 Radiant/Necrotic damage) OR Potent Spellcasting (add your Wisdom modifier to Cantrip damage).

Divine Intervention - Level 10

As a Magic Action you can automatically cast ANY spell from the Cleric spell list of 5th level or lower, without expending a slot or components. You cannot use this ability again until you finish a Long Rest.

Improved Blessed Strikes - Level 14

If you have Divine Strike: damage increases to 2d8. If Potent Spellcasting: when you deal Cantrip damage, you or an ally within 60 ft gain Temporary HP equal to double your Wisdom modifier.

Greater Divine Intervention - Level 20

When you use Divine Intervention, you can choose the spell Wish from the spell list. If you do, you cannot use Divine Intervention again for 2d4 Long Rests.

Cleric Domains

Servants of healing gods focused on the positive energy that sustains life.

Key Class Features

Spellcasting and Language - Level 1

You use Wisdom for spellcasting and can use a Druidic focus (e.g. a sprig of mistletoe). Druids prepare their spell list each day during a Long Rest.

Druidic: You know the secret language of druids, which can be used to leave hidden messages. Through it you always have Speak with Animals prepared.

Primal Order - Level 1

Choose your role:
Magician: You know 1 additional Cantrip. You gain a bonus to Arcana or Nature checks equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum +1).
Warden: You gain proficiency with Martial weapons and Medium armor.

Wild Shape - Level 2

As a Bonus Action, you can transform into a Beast form you know. You know 4 Beast forms (they can be changed after a Long Rest).

  • Form limitations: At 2nd level max CR 1/4, no flying speed. At 4th level max CR 1/2. At 8th level max CR 1, and you can fly.
  • Duration: A number of hours equal to half your Druid level.
  • Hit Points: You take on the beast's stats but keep your mental scores (INT, WIS, CHA) and skills. When you transform you gain Temporary HP equal to your Druid level.

Wild Companion - Level 2

As a Magic action, you can expend a spell slot OR one Wild Shape use to cast Find Familiar (no material components). The familiar has the Fey type and disappears after a Long Rest.

Wild Resurgence - Level 5

Once on your turn, if you have no Wild Shape uses, you can sacrifice a spell slot to regain 1 use. You can also sacrifice a Wild Shape once per day to regain a 1st-level spell slot.

Elemental Fury - Level 7

Choose one: Potent Spellcasting (add Wisdom modifier to Cantrip damage) OR Primal Strike (once per turn when hitting with a weapon or beast attack, deal an extra 1d8 Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Thunder damage).

Improved Elemental Fury - Level 15

Spellcasting: Cantrips with a range of 10+ ft increase their range by 300 feet. Strike: The extra damage increases to 2d8.

Beast Spells - Level 18
